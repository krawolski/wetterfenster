Questions hikers ask

Where does the forecast data come from?

From the MeteoSwiss ICON-CH1 model - for Swiss locations via the official MeteoSwiss sources, for border areas via Open-Meteo running the same model. It's the highest-resolution forecast available for the Alps.

What exactly is a "weather window"?

The mountaineering term for the stretch of hours good enough to be out on the mountain. You tell Wetterfenster your hours (say 07–16); it finds the valleys where those hours are sunny, dry and storm-free.

Why valley gateways and not summits?

Each of the 168 locations is the village where a hiking valley starts - where you park. Forecasts are most reliable there, and every trailhead above it shares that valley's weather character.

Is my drive time really calculated from my home?

Yes - set your home base once (or use geolocation) and every ranking is sorted with real driving estimates from your door. That's the point: the best weather you can actually reach.

Do I need an account?

No. The free ranking works without any sign-up. An optional account later unlocks Pro features like saved bases and alerts.
